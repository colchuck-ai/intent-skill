# Plain language (for all intent docs)

Readers include **junior engineers** and people new to the domain. The goal is **clarity**, not impressing anyone. If a sentence sounds like it is showing off vocabulary or insider cleverness, rewrite it.

## Do

- Use **short sentences** and **common words**. Prefer “use” over “leverage,” “because” over “insofar as,” “change” over “mutate the narrative.”
- Say **what**, **who**, and **why** in plain terms before jargon. Introduce a term once, then use it consistently.
- Prefer **concrete** wording: examples, boundaries, and verifiable statements over abstract throat-clearing.
- Write like you are **helping a teammate ship**, not grading them.

## Avoid

- **Performative tone**—sentences that mainly signal “I am sophisticated” (stacked clauses, rare words, ironic distance).
- **Unnecessary hedging** that pads length without adding precision (“it is worth noting that,” “one might argue that”).
- **Vague grandeur** (“holistic,” “synergize,” “leverage key learnings”) where a specific verb and object would do.
- **Condescension**—plain language is not talking down; it is being direct and respectful.

## Sessions

Workflow files under [`references/workflows/`](../workflows/README.md) weave this guide into **draft**, **AI-assisted review**, and **independent review** in the same narrative as the rest of the session—apply it while drafting; in review passes, plain readability sits beside the substantive checks (same bar as the questions below), not as an isolated checklist.

## Review pass

When editing or reviewing any intent artifact, ask:

1. Would a **new hire on the team** understand this without re-reading?
2. Can any paragraph be **split** or **simplified** without losing meaning?
3. Is every **unusual word** necessary, or is there a simpler one?

If the honest answer to (1) is no, revise before you merge.
